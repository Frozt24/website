International Buisness Machines Corporation (IBMC)

International Buisness Machines Corporation (commonly referred to as IBM) is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Armonk, New York , United States , with operations in over 170 countries. The company originated in 1911 as the computing-Tabulating-Recording Company (CTR) and was renamed "International Buisness Machines" in 1924.

IBM has continually shifted its buisness mix by exiting commoditizing markets and focusing on higher-value, more profitable markets. this includes spinning off printer manufacturer Lexmark in 1991 and selling off its personal computer (ThinkPad) and x86-based server business to Lenovo (2005 and 2014) and acquiring companies such as PWC consulting, SPSS , and The Weather Company (2016).

Nicknamed Big Blue. IBM isnone of 30 companies included in the Dow Jones Industrial Average and one of the world's largest employers, with (as of 2016) nearly 380,000 employees, known as "IBMERS", IBM employees have been awaeded five nobel prizes, six Turing Awards, ten National medals of Technology and five National Medals of Science
